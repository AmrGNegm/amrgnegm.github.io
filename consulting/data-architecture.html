Data Integration Architecture

Connect your eCommerce, CRM, CX, and pricing systems into one governed, reliable data platform — from design to operation.

Your data lives in too many places.

Banking platforms, point-of-sale systems, CRM databases, CX operations, pricing engines — every enterprise has dozens of data sources that need to speak the same language. When they don’t, you get conflicting reports, slow decisions, and teams that don’t trust the numbers.

I design and deliver the integration architecture that makes all your data sources work as one platform — a true single source of truth.

What I Deliver

  • Multi-system integration design: Architecture that connects eCommerce, CRM, CX, and pricing data sources into a unified pipeline
  • Azure Data Factory pipelines: Production-grade ADF pipelines with proper orchestration, monitoring, and error handling
  • Star schema data warehouse: Dimensional modelling that delivers fast, flexible analytics across all business units
  • Medallion architecture (Bronze/Silver/Gold): Structured data lake layers that balance raw ingenuity with business-ready outputs
  • Microsoft Fabric workloads: Lakehouse, Datawarehouse, Dataflows Gen2, and DirectLake mode for modern analytics
  • Data governance & SSOT: Data quality rules, lineage tracking, and governance frameworks that make your data trustworthy
  • End-to-end Power BI dashboards: Self-service and executive dashboards built on your governed data platform

How I Work

1. Discover. I map your data sources, identify integration points, and document quality issues and business requirements.

2. Architect. I design the target architecture — pipeline topology, data model, governance rules, and dashboards — with your team’s buy-in at every step.

3. Build. I implement the full platform: ADF pipelines, DWH, data quality checks, and Power BI reports. Everything is version-controlled and documented.

4. Operate & Hand Over. I monitor the platform in production, train your team, and hand over a fully documented system that runs reliably.
